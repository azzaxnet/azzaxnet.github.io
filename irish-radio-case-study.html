Case study

Irish Radios

Do you sometimes feel overwhelmed by the millions of songs to choose from on Spotify, or do you feel like not listening to podcasts when you’re cooking or on the bus? Have you ever lived in another country, or do you just have a craze for another culture? Then a simple and easy solution could be to listen to radio from faraway, maybe through an App.

The problem

For me that App was Irish Radios. Even though I got my dose of Irish radio, this particular App had its vast weaknesses. Therefore, I took on the design challenge to improve its usability. The App was built on a simple idea, but the user flow was constantly interrupted and the design looked outdated. With this redesign, the App would be able to compete better in its segment.

The user flow of the original App design. You have to take 5 actions from entering the App until you can start listening to the radio.

The process

I did the redesign in form of a one-week design sprint based on the Design Thinking methodology empathize, define, ideate, prototype and test. By doing this, I managed to improve the App in a rapid and efficient way.

The design sprint was done only by me, but with great help from a small group of friends that acted as potential users and gave me feedback during the short run of the project.

The project started with some short interviews about how, when and why someone would even use a radio App. After gathering the background information I let the interviewees give the existing App a go and I did a contextual inquiry where I could observe their pain points and let them react to the App. I decided on doing first interviews and then the contextual inquiries because it was a good way to get the users perceived point of views, followed by their real actions with the product. From this exercise, I found that the users liked the simplicity of the App together with its quick access, even though they felt constantly interrupted by pop up messages. A common pain point was also that the interface looked both dated and “amateurish”.

With this research done, I created 3 proto-personas and customer journey maps to help me define the scope of the redesign. This helped me focus the design on the simplicity of the App which made it more accessible and easier to navigate.

One of three proto-personas and Customer Journey Map.

During the ideation stage, I used the crazy-8 method to quickly come up with ideas for both layout and functionalities that would be used in the later prototypes. One of the pain points, the difficulty to find a specific radio station, would, in the end, be solved by using different ways of sorting the station list, a new feature to add favourites as well as a function to search the stations within the App. All these options were in the end important to have it clear and accessible for the user.

Brainstorming with Crazy 8, and creating low fidelity paper mockups.

From the alternatives made from my brainstorming exercise, I developed a low fidelity mock-up in Figma that I tested on the users that tried the original App. The response was in general very positive, but I had to make a few amendments and twists to adopt them to wireframes which I used to create a high fidelity mock-up (that one also in Figma)

First set of mock-ups, later used as wireframes after being adopted from the users' input.

Final outcome

Final redesign. The radio station list is always easy accessible, even when you are listening to another station. There is also different ways to find stations as well as an option to save your favourites.

The redesign of the Irish Radios App had a very positive impact on the user panel I used during the project. All users found it much easier to use and the layout led to a lower feeling of cognitive load when they tested the high-fidelity prototype.

By doing this project without access to real users, the result of the empathize stage will not be as accurate as it could be, but the unison response from my test users make me believe that the redesign would be a relevant improvement to the current App. The next stage would be to do real tests on users to show the owner of the App the benefits of applying the changes to their product.

While doing this project I really strengthened my belief in the usefulness of Design Thinking as an approach, as it's a fantastic system for putting the user in the centre and break free from your own biases.
